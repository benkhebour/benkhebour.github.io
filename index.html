Common Ground Tracking in Multimodal Dialogue

Developed a novel multimodal Common-Ground tracking framework for collaborative dialogue, integrating linguistic (speech) and nonverbal cues (prosody, gestures, actions, and collaboration cues) to model shared beliefs and questions under discussion. Through this project I introduced a benchmark on the Weights Task Dataset and evaluated how each modality contributes for collaborative reasoning. LREC-COLING 2024

Detection of collaborative states using multimodal features

Developed a multimodal model for automatic detection of collaborative states in small-group interactions, integrating linguistic and behavioral cues to capture group dynamics such as confusion, coordination, and agreement. This work highlights the role of multimodal signals in enabling adaptive, group-aware AI systems for collaborative learning. AIED 2023

Non-verbal feature contributions to multimodal interpretation of meaning

Developed a multimodal framework for modeling and predicting collaboration quality in group learning environments, integrating behavioral and interaction signals to infer engagement and satisfaction. Evaluated how multimodal features support adaptive, data-driven interventions for collaborative learning.

Loanword detection

Developed a generalized multilingual framework for automatic loanword detection, integrating phonetic, semantic, and textual similarity using multilingual language models. Introduced the WikLoW dataset across diverse language pairs and demonstrated strong cross-lingual generalization, achieving performance comparable to or exceeding human baselines. COLING 2022

How Good is Automatic Segmentation as a Multimodal Discourse Annotation Aid?

Investigated the impact of automatic speech segmentation on multimodal discourse annotation, benchmarking ASR-generated utterances against gold-standard transcripts. Revealed systematic misalignment and annotation inconsistencies, providing insights for designing robust multimodal annotation pipelines. ISA 2023
